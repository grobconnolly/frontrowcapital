Methodology

Player earnings data covers 2012–2044 (FanGraphs Top 100 prospect lists from 2012, 2013, and 2014; MLB salaries from baseball-reference / Spotrac). Pick a single-vintage (2012, 2013, or 2014) to draw 30 random prospects from that year's Top 100, or pick 3-Year to model a 2012-vintage fund deploying across a 3-year investment period (2012–2014). In 3-year mode each year is treated as an independent draft. The per-year cadence is randomized each roll: three integers in [3, 18] summing to 30. For each year the rank filter (Full 100 / P10–P90 / P25–P75) is applied to that year's Top 100, then the per-year batch is drawn at random from that year's eligible pool. After all three drafts, the simulator scans for cross-year duplicates (same player on two or more lists). For each duplicate the earlier year's slot is replaced with a fresh random pick from that year's eligible pool — excluding everyone already on the combined roster — and the dedupe scan repeats until clean. Each pick costs $283K for 5% of their MLB career earnings; capital is called in the year each player is signed, so 3-year mode's capital calls are staggered across 2012–2014. The 30 picks together total the $8.5M ISA portfolio. Fund life is a 12-year base term plus three 1-year extensions (15 years total). Management fees ($170K/yr in years 1–3, $42.5K/yr in years 4–15) are charged on the $8.5M ISA portfolio basis from fund inception (2012). Distributions follow a European whole-fund waterfall on the ISA portfolio: 100% to LPs until $8.5M of ISA capital is returned, then 80% LP / 20% GP. Net LP IRR is computed via bisection on the LP's actual cash-flow stream (capital calls less distributions in each fund-year). Backtest only — past performance is not indicative of future results.
