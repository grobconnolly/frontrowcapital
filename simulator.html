One Deal, Three Moves
1
Sign

The fund pays 28 top prospects $300K apiece, theirs to keep regardless. Each owes back 4% of every major-league paycheck for the length of his career. A player who never reaches the majors owes nothing — the fund wears the miss.

2
Collect

Paychecks arrive for 15 years, and you stand first in line: 100% of distributions until your capital is returned, then 80% of everything after. At year 15, positions still paying are sold to a successor fund and the proceeds distributed. One wire upfront; no capital calls, ever.

3
Replay

A backtest, not a forecast. Pick a draft era and roll — a random roster from the actual FanGraphs lists, the careers that followed, run through the fund's terms. Every bust counted, every fee out. Deal another hand whenever you doubt one.

Methodology, in Brief
  • Real players, real paychecks. Rosters draw from the 2005–2016 FanGraphs Top 100s; collections are the salaries actually paid, per Baseball-Reference and Spotrac, in the dollars of the day.
  • Era-honest pricing. The $300K / 4% deal is quoted in 2026 dollars and deflated to each vintage — roughly $175K a pick for a 2005 fund — and your commitment translates the same way on entry. The payback schedule runs in the era's dollars; the headline distribution restates it in today's, so commitment × multiple is the headline figure.
  • Fifteen years, hard stop. Collections end at year 15; positions still paying are sold to a successor fund for a discounted lump sum. No LP waits on a career that outruns the fund.
  • LPs are paid first. 100% of distributions until capital is returned, then 80/20. Fees run 2% in years 1–3 and 0.5% thereafter; your outcome above is net of both.
  • A backtest, not a forecast. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Confidential — for discussion with accredited investors only.

Full Methodology

Player earnings data covers the 2005–2016 FanGraphs Top 100 prospect lists (MLB salaries from baseball-reference / Spotrac), with each player's earnings aligned to his prospect year. Salaries flow through the model as raw nominal, year-of-payment dollars — the actual paychecks the player received — matching how a real-world investor of that vintage would have experienced the cash flows. Pick any single vintage (2005–2016) to draw 28 random prospects from that year's Top 100, or switch to 3-Year Deploy and pick a start year to model a fund deploying across three consecutive lists (start year through start+2; start years run 2005–2014). In 3-year mode each year is treated as an independent draft. The per-year cadence is randomized each roll: three integers in [3, 18] summing to 28, with each year additionally capped at its eligible pool size. For each year the rank filter (Full 100 / P1–P50 / P25–P75 / P50–P100) is applied to that year's Top 100, then the per-year batch is drawn at random from that year's eligible pool. After all three drafts, the simulator scans for cross-year duplicates (same player on two or more lists). For each duplicate the earlier year's slot is replaced with a fresh random pick from that year's eligible pool — excluding everyone already on the combined roster — and the dedupe scan repeats until clean. Deal terms are quoted in 2026 dollars — $300K per signing for 4% of MLB career earnings, spread evenly across 28 picks on the $8.4M sleeve — and then deflated to the fund's inception year using the Front Row CPI index. A 2005-vintage fund therefore invests ≈$4.9M nominal (28 picks × ≈$175K in 2005 dollars); a 2016 vintage invests ≈$6.0M; a hypothetical 2026 vintage invests the full $8.4M. In 3-year deploy mode signings are spread across the three vintage years, so the basis uses the simple average of those years' deflators (a 2005–2007 deploy invests ≈$5.05M nominal). Your commitment is quoted in today's dollars and translated to the fund's vintage year on entry (a $1M commitment to a 2005 fund wires ≈$583K of 2005 money). The payback schedule runs in those vintage-year dollars — the cash flows an investor of that era actually experienced — while the headline distribution restates the same outcome in today's dollars at the entry rate, so commitment × MOIC equals the headline figure. MOIC, IRR, and payback timing are identical in both frames. Fund-level waterfall figures remain in the vintage's nominal dollars. Narrow rank filters can cap the roster below 28 (repeat prospects across deploy years are dropped); when that happens the fund scales to the capital actually deployed — picks × the vintage-year price — and management fees and the return-of-capital hurdle are computed on that smaller basis, so returns stay comparable across filters. The full invested amount is wired upfront at fund inception (the selected start year) — there are no capital calls, even in 3-year mode where players are signed across the three deploy years. Fund life is a 12-year base term plus three 1-year extensions (15 years total). Management fees (2%/yr in years 1–3, 0.5%/yr in years 4–15) are charged on the invested ISA basis from fund inception and are denominated in that vintage year's dollars. The 15-year term is a hard stop on the schedule: ISA collections in years 1–15 are realized live, and every position still earning after the term (a player's career can run to 2044) is sold at the end of year 15 — to a successor Front Row fund or to private investors — for the present value of its remaining collections, discounted at a 12% secondary-buyer required return. Those sale proceeds are realized in year 15 and run through the same waterfall, so no LP cash flow falls outside the fund's life. Distributions follow a European whole-fund waterfall on the ISA portfolio: 100% to LPs until the invested ISA capital is returned, then 80% LP / 20% GP. Net LP MOIC and IRR are nominal — computed on the actual year-by-year cash-flow stream (full upfront investment in Year 1, distributions in each fund-year through year 15), un-adjusted for inflation. For old vintages this includes inflation-driven return; a real (inflation-adjusted) IRR would run several points lower. Backtest only — past performance is not indicative of future results.
